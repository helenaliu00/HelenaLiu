Offline Algorithmic Thinking

Generative art refers to any art practice where the artist creates a process such as a set of natural language rules, a computer program, a machine, or other procedural invention, which is then set into motion with some degree of autonomy contributing to or resulting in a completed work of art.
List of Artists to keep in mind:

For this exercise, we covered the concept of enacting out one day of our lives according to the chance of a coin or dice. I created my parameters according to the flip of a coin. These are the parameters I set up and came out with for the 14th of April (Sunday):

  • 1. Wake up at 7am (H) or at 9am (T)?
    Heads: groggily crawled out of bed and regretting my life decision to be a night owl.
  • 2. Get up and eat breakfast (H) or scroll for a while on social media (T)?
    Heads: time to be productive!
  • 3. Eat noodles (H) or eat the leftover woolies scones (T)?
    Heads: I prefer having a larger, savoury brekkie!
  • 4. Do some gaming (H) or drawing for a bit (T)?
    Tails: have to keep ontop of the content grind.
  • 5. Eating lunch - do I scour my empty fridge looking for what I can make (H), or go out and buy something (T)?
    Heads: (Again??) made a very strange tunamayo sandwich.
  • 6. Time to study, and catch up on assignments. Do I stay at home (H) or go to the library (T) to study?
    Tails: Better concentration is the way to go!

Admittedly towards the later part of the evening after coming back from Parkville, I may have mostly given up the motivation to really carry out the idea.
